The History of Transformers

Brand Origin

The theme of transformers wasn’t the original idea. In 1980, “Takara Toys” launched the Dicaclone toy line which were figures of robots and vehicals. Unlike the modern transforms, these robots wern’t alive and were piloted by micro figures. Two years later, “Takara Toys” released a toy line of car-robots which can be converted mech to vechical and the other way around.

At the “Tokyo Toy Show” in 1983, “Hasbro” bought the rights and molds to the 28 figures in the Diaclone toy line to import to North America, which would be remade into the transformers, consisting of 18 autobots and 10 decepticons. Working with Marvel editors, “Hasbro” created the backstory and names of the transformer charaters along with breif descriptions, forming the fantasy world of “Transformers.”

Figures to Shows

To introduce the backstory and charaters in “Transformers,” a three-part cartoon series animated by “Toei Animation” was aired on September 1984, which would be something kids would hold to while growing up. Between the second and third seasons, the first transformers movie came out on 8/8/1986. While it stired a lot of emotion in kids, the movie introduced new charaters in the series and reversions for the 1986 toy release.

In 2007, Micheal Bay directed the first live-action Transformer movie with “Paramount Pichtures”, which won four Oscar rewards. Bay directed follow up movies over the years, Revenge of the Fallen in 2009, Dark of the Moon in 2011, Age of Extinction in 2014, and the latest installment, The Last Knight in 2017.

Universe

In the fictional universe of “Transformers” two factions of living machines, the heroic autobots and the evil decepticons, left their metalic planet of Cybertron to search for alternate energy scources to revitalize their war efforts. After millions of years of laying dormant on Earth, they reveal themselfs as the autobots defend humanity and their new home from the decepticons.

Characters

Optimus Prime

Leader of the autobots. Optimus Prime is a noble leader to his team of autobots. Compassion, Optimus believes that all sentient beings has the right for freedom and fights for that future. Carrying the matrix of leadership, Optimus has a vast knowledge of Cybertron’s tech and history.

Bumblebee

Autobot scout and one of Optimus Prime’s most trusted allies. Bumblebee is often underestimated when his enimies judge him by his size. But he has proven himself worthy throught the battles and will contiune to fight for the good cause.

Grimlock

The dinobot king. Grimlock wields legendary strength that makes him unmatched in battle. Even though he respects strength above all else, Grimlock hates bullies like the decepticons and that’s what motievates him to fight alongside the autobots aganist the forces of evil.

Megatron

The leader of the decepticon army. Megatron is a merciless being and one of the most powerful and feared warriors from Cybertron. Driven by a never-ending hunger for conquest, Megatron is willing to destroy anything or anyone who stands in the way of his evil goals, including arch enemy Optimus Prime.

Starscream

Second-in-command and air-commander of the decepticons. Like many other of the decepticons, Starscream is unsurpassed in ego or ambition. Scheming, he seeks to upseat Megatron from the leader of the decepticons.
